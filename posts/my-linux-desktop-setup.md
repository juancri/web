## Introduction

I've been using Linux for more than 20 years now. It took me several years to make Linux my primary desktop driver, though. Here's a snapshot of my current setup, from the distro to the tools I use for software development.

## The distro

My first distro was [SuSE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SUSE_Linux) 5.1. After a while, I used [Red Hat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Hat_Linux), [Debian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Debian), [Gentoo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gentoo_Linux) and [Ubuntu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ubuntu).

It was not easy to use Linux during the late 90s and early 2000s as my primary desktop environment. When laptops stated to be a thing, simple things like connecting to a new wireless network was a not-easy task on Linux. Ubuntu changed that and it was really thought as a nice desktop experience. That was the point when I decided to fully move from Windows to Linux.

After Ubuntu, I jumped to [Arch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arch_Linux) and I currently use several variants of it, but mainly *vanilla* Arch.

## The desktop

My favorite desktop environment is [MATE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MATE_(software)) (pronounced *ma-te* as the [drink](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mate_(drink)) in Spanish). It might be that I'm just old and I miss the early 2000s, but MATE + [compiz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compiz) are usually the base I choose for my desktop.

I use MATE but that does not mean that I don't like modern desktop experiences. That's why I use [synapse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Synapse_(software)) and [plank](https://wiki.archlinux.org/index.php/Plank) as the launcher and dock, respectively.
